## Understanding Sex and Gender (2 items)

**1.** A college student wears makeup and dresses in a way that others read as feminine, but he knows himself to be a man and is attracted to women. The makeup and clothing are an example of his —

- A. gender expression **(correct)**
- B. gender identity
- C. sexual orientation
- D. sexual identity

*Correct: A.* Gender expression is the outward presentation of gender through things like clothing and makeup, separate from a person's internal sense of self or who they are attracted to.

**2.** The textbook compares one way of thinking about gender to a dimmer switch that can rest at any point between fully bright and fully dim. This image is meant to illustrate which idea?

- A. Gender can fall anywhere along a range of possibilities **(correct)**
- B. Gender is set permanently before birth
- C. Gender comes in only two opposite types
- D. Gender is decided entirely by anatomy

*Correct: A.* A dimmer, unlike an on/off switch, can rest at countless settings, mirroring the idea that gender is a spectrum with many valid points rather than two fixed options.

## Prenatal Sex Differentiation (2 items)

**3.** If the testes are removed from an XY embryo before they start producing hormones, how will that embryo's body develop?

- A. As female **(correct)**
- B. As male
- C. With both male and female reproductive tracts
- D. Without any reproductive structures

*Correct: A.* Female development is the default pathway that unfolds when the male hormone signals never turn on, so an XY embryo without working testes develops a female body.

**4.** The penis and the clitoris develop from the same starting tissue in the embryo. Body parts that share a common embryonic origin are called —

- A. homologous organs **(correct)**
- B. analogous organs
- C. reproductive gonads
- D. sex-linked organs

*Correct: A.* Homologous organs arise from the same embryonic tissue even when they end up looking or working differently, as with the penis and clitoris.

## Puberty as a Second Differentiation (1 item)

**5.** A 16-year-old competitive distance runner who is extremely lean has not yet had her first period. Which explanation best fits the hypothesis described in the chapter?

- A. She has not stored enough fat for her body to begin its cycle **(correct)**
- B. Her low weight has caused her ovaries to stop forming eggs
- C. Intense running raises hormones that block the first period
- D. Lean athletes tend to inherit later first periods

*Correct: A.* The percent body fat hypothesis holds that menstruation cannot begin until a girl has stored enough body fat (about 17–22%) to support a possible pregnancy.

## Intersex Variations (2 items)

**6.** A teenager who has always identified as a girl sees a doctor because she has not begun menstruating. Testing shows she has XY chromosomes and internal testes that make normal testosterone, yet her body developed as female. Which condition best fits?

- A. Androgen Insensitivity Syndrome **(correct)**
- B. Congenital Adrenal Hyperplasia
- C. 5-Alpha Reductase Deficiency
- D. Klinefelter syndrome

*Correct: A.* In AIS, an XY body produces testosterone but cannot respond to it, so it develops as female and the condition often surfaces when an expected first period never arrives.

**7.** What do major medical organizations now recommend about operating on the genitals of intersex infants?

- A. Hold off on any medically unnecessary surgery until the person can decide **(correct)**
- B. Carry out corrective surgery as soon as possible after birth
- C. Raise every such child with no gender assigned at all
- D. Let the parents pick whichever surgery they prefer

*Correct: A.* Because early surgeries often cause lasting physical and psychological harm, the current standard is to defer non-essential genital surgery until the individual can give consent.

## Gender Differences and Similarities (1 item)

**8.** Men and women in one study reported very different numbers of past sexual partners, but those differences shrank when participants thought they were hooked up to a working lie detector. What does this result most likely show?

- A. Part of the reported gap reflects people answering the way they think they should **(correct)**
- B. Men and women turn out to have identical sexual histories
- C. Lie detectors are a dependable way to measure sexual behavior
- D. People are unable to recall their own past behavior accurately

*Correct: A.* When participants believed lies would be caught, the gap narrowed, suggesting some reported differences come from social pressure to answer a certain way rather than from real differences in behavior.

## Where Do Gender Differences Come From? (2 items)

**9.** A researcher argues that because females put far more biological resources into each pregnancy than males do, females should be choosier about partners. This argument rests on which idea?

- A. Parental investment theory **(correct)**
- B. Gender schema theory
- C. Social learning theory
- D. The gender similarities hypothesis

*Correct: A.* Parental investment theory predicts that the sex contributing more to each offspring will be more selective about mates, which in this case means females.

**10.** A young child is sure that dolls are for girls and trucks are for boys, and concludes that a boy carrying a doll is "doing it wrong." The mental framework she uses to make that judgment is best called a —

- A. gender schema **(correct)**
- B. sexual script
- C. gender binary
- D. social learning

*Correct: A.* A gender schema is the personal mental map of masculine and feminine that a child builds and then uses to interpret and judge new situations.
